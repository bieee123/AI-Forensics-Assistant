RUNBOOK: General Incident Response Framework

Phases:
1. Detection & Identification
2. Containment
3. Eradication
4. Recovery
5. Post-Incident Review

Phase 1 — Detection & Identification:
- Confirm the incident is real and not a false positive
- Determine incident type: intrusion, malware, insider threat, data breach, denial of service
- Assign an incident severity level (Critical/High/Medium/Low)
- Assign an incident commander and open an incident ticket
- Begin timeline documentation — every action taken must be timestamped

Phase 2 — Containment:
- Short-term: isolate affected systems to prevent further spread
- Long-term: implement network-level controls (firewall rules, VLAN isolation)
- Preserve forensic evidence before containment actions modify system state
- Communicate containment actions to IT operations to avoid business disruption

Phase 3 — Eradication:
- Remove malicious artifacts: malware, backdoors, unauthorized accounts, persistence mechanisms
- Patch the vulnerability that allowed the incident to occur
- Reset all credentials that may have been compromised
- Verify eradication is complete before proceeding to recovery

Phase 4 — Recovery:
- Restore affected systems from known-good backups
- Monitor restored systems closely for 72 hours for signs of re-compromise
- Gradually restore normal operations with enhanced monitoring in place
- Communicate recovery status to stakeholders

Phase 5 — Post-Incident Review:
- Conduct a post-mortem within 5 business days
- Document: what happened, how it was detected, what was done, what could be improved
- Update runbooks and detection rules based on lessons learned
- Track remediation items to completion

IoC Documentation:
All indicators of compromise identified during the incident must be documented:
- IP addresses involved
- File hashes (SHA-256) of malicious artifacts
- Domain names used by attackers
- Attack techniques mapped to MITRE ATT&CK framework
